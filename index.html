Abstract

Mathematical literacy (ML) portraits, which characterize students' mathematical competence across multiple dimensions, play a crucial role in personalized education. Recently, the Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) released the latest Program for International Student Assessment (PISA) data, employing the latest framework which specifically focuses on ML. However, constructing comprehensive student portraits (SPs) in ML remains challenging, as many existing popular methods fail to capture the complex interrelationships among the diverse dimensions of ML. Therefore, we propose a novel method, called Constructing Student Portraits in ML with Subdivision Graph Neural Networks (CSP-SGNN), for predicting holistic ML and to give personalized suggestions to improve student's ML. Specifically, we use hierarchical graph embedding that represents the relationship between mathematical reasoning, the problem-solving cycle, and mathematical contents and further apply the self-attention mechanism to adaptively emphasize critical nodes and edges. Extensive experiments on real-world datasets demonstrate that CSP-SGNN outperforms existing popular methods. Furthermore, our approach validates the effectiveness of PISA's ML framework while providing interpretable SPs for personalized learning..
